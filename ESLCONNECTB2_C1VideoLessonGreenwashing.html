B2/C1 Grammar: Gerunds and Infinitives for Sustainability Claims

This section practises using gerunds and infinitives to discuss greenwashing, consumer trust, regulation and corporate responsibility.

Useful language: avoid making vague claims / promise to reduce emissions / risk misleading consumers / need to provide evidence

Grammar Section 1: Choose the Correct Form

Choose an answer to get instant green/red feedback.

2. Regulators want companies ___ environmental claims with scientific evidence.

Grammar Section 3: Useful Patterns

PatternExampleUse
avoid + gerundCompanies should avoid making vague claims.Say what should not happen.
risk + gerundBrands risk losing consumer trust.Describe possible negative consequences.
promise + infinitiveThe company promised to reduce emissions.Describe future commitments.
need + infinitiveFirms need to provide scientific evidence.Describe requirements.
accuse someone of + gerundCritics accused the brand of misleading customers.Report criticism.
Task: Give advice to a company that wants to avoid greenwashing. Use at least three gerunds and three infinitives.

Thought-Provoking Questions

1. Why is greenwashing dangerous for consumers?

2. Should companies be punished for vague sustainability claims?

3. Are carbon offsets a useful tool or a distraction?

4. How can consumers check whether a product is genuinely sustainable?

5. Should all environmental claims require scientific evidence?

6. Why do companies prefer global rules rather than different rules in each market?

7. Is it better for companies to make imperfect progress or avoid claims until they are fully sustainable?
