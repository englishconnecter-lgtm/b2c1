Lesson Aim

Students watch a BBC Ideas video about working with people they dislike and practise language for workplace conflict, difficult conversations, rapport-building and challenging assumptions.

B2/C1 Grammar: Difficult Conversations at Work

This section practises diplomatic language for describing behaviour, avoiding blame and summarising another person’s viewpoint.

Useful language: When this happened, I felt... / Could I check I’ve understood? / What I’m hearing is... / On this occasion...

Thought-Provoking Questions

2. Why do people avoid difficult conversations?

3. What is the difference between being honest and being rude?

4. How can quiet people be misunderstood at work?

5. Why is summarising someone’s view powerful?

6. Should workplace conflict always be discussed directly?

7. What assumptions might people make about you at work?

Context

Situation: Two colleagues are in tension because of an irritating workplace habit. They need to have an honest but respectful conversation.
