B2/C1 Grammar: Managing Online Meeting Problems

This section practises polite requests, clarification questions and cause/result language for remote-work problems.

Useful language: Can you hear me? / Could you try...? / I’m having trouble... / Because the connection is unstable, we may need to...

Grammar Section 1: Polite Requests

Choose an answer to get instant green/red feedback.

1. Which request sounds most professional?

2. Complete: “Could you try ___ your output settings?”

Grammar Section 2: Clarifying Technical Problems

Grammar Section 3: Cause and Result

ProblemResultProfessional response
The connection is unstable.People miss information.Could you repeat the key figures?
The platform needs an update.People join late.Let’s allow extra time before future meetings.
There is echo.People cannot hear clearly.Could you use headphones or mute your speaker?
Speaking task: Describe three meeting problems from the video using cause/result language: Because..., As a result..., This means that...

Thought-Provoking Questions

1. Are online meetings more efficient or more stressful than face-to-face meetings?

2. Who is responsible when technology fails: the employee or the company?

3. Should people join online meetings five minutes early?

4. What rules should every online meeting have?

5. When should a meeting be rescheduled instead of continued?

6. How can managers include people who are joining by phone?

7. What is the most annoying online meeting habit?

Context

Situation: Your team is trying to discuss an important financial report, but there are sound problems, late arrivals and background interruptions.

Student A

You are the meeting leader. Keep the meeting calm, solve technical problems and decide whether to continue.

Student B

You are a team member with connection problems. Explain what is happening and ask people to repeat key information.

Role play task: Manage the meeting for five minutes. By the end, decide whether to continue, reschedule or switch to a conference call.
