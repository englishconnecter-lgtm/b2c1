B2/C1 ESL Connect Video lesson based on the tapescript: youth activism, constitutional rights, climate litigation and environmental policy.

Lesson Aim

Students watch a CBS News interview about young climate activists winning a Montana climate case and practise language for legal arguments, constitutional rights, climate policy and youth activism.

Before watching

  1. Can young people influence climate policy?
  2. Should a clean environment be a constitutional right?
  3. What role should courts play in climate change cases?

Task: Summarise the Montana climate case using five reported-speech sentences: one from the judge, one from a plaintiff, one from the lawyer, one from the state officials and one from the journalist.

Thought-Provoking Questions

1. Should children and teenagers be able to bring climate cases to court?

2. Is a clean and healthful environment a basic right?

3. Should courts or politicians lead climate action?

4. Why might state constitutions be important in climate cases?

5. Can one legal case influence national policy?

6. What can young people do before they are old enough to vote?

7. What responsibilities do governments have towards future generations?
